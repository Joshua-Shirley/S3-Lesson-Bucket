Children Specialist Reference Guide

Play Keep it fun, interactive, and playful. On their level, this is going to be a great day of fun ahead.

Drill NOT drill sergeant! It means presenting and sharing information in a way that is experiential. Create fun, skill-specific activities that develop positive movement patterns.

Adventure Take the skills the children have learned and apply them in new and exciting ways! Delay new skill presentation. Solidify new skills as you explore the mountain.

Summary Review the day and help the children remember / recall what they did.

Fundamentals

Alpine Skiing Fundamentals

Ski Skills describe how to ski interacts with the snow. The connection between the snow and ski.

Fundamentals are descriptions of how the skier's movements (body) affect the ski.

Ski Skill Alpine Ski Fundamental
Pressure Control the relationship of the center of mass to the base of support to direct pressure along the length of the skis.
Pressure Control pressure from ski to ski and direct pressure toward the outside ski.
Edging Control edge angles through a combination of inclination and angulation.
Rotation Control the skis' rotation with leg rotation, separate from the upper body.
Pressure Regulate the magnitude of pressure created through ski/snow interation.
Teaching Fundamentals
  • Assess and Plan: Collaborate on long-term goals and short-term objectives.
  • Manage Information, activities, terrain selection, and pacing.
  • Promote play, experimentation, and exploration.
  • Facilitate the learner's ability to reflect upon experiences and sensations.
  • Adapt to the changing needs of the learner.
  • Manage emotional and physical risk.
